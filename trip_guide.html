Trip Guide

I have some tips that I think will help anyone thinking about visiting a theme park.

Before you go

  • Buy your tickets online - they are always cheaper online and you can skip the purchase line at the gate.
  • If the park you are visiting is far and it's not likely you'll be back for a while, consider the fast lane offering if there are any. They can be expensive, but unless you plan on a few days at the park, sometimes it's the only way to get on every ride during your visit.
  • Check the weather to be aware, but unless you see a full day of thunderstroms in the morning forecast, don't rush to cancelling your trip. A little rain in the forecast can actually mean the park won't be that busy, but some rides may close for even a drop. Others will remain completely open as long as there is no hail, excessive wind, or lightning. If you don't mind a little rain it can work out. Thunderstorms can be unpredictable. Sometimes there is rain when none is forecasted and sometimes it's clear skies when it said there would be rain. If you have a lot of flexibility on when you visit, then go when the weather will be clear but depending on if you have friends or family joining you, that only have this day set aside, you might have to stick to a schedule.
  • Plan how you will carry your belongings. Use a small waist pack to carry the few things you do bring. Alternatively, make sure you have pockets that close very securely with some kind of fastener, making sure things can't slip out. Do not wear hats on roller coasters. if you plan on wearing glasses, have a snug lanyard or be mindful to only face forward. If you know a ride has a lot of "ejector" airtime, or inverted "hangtime" seriously consider not bringing glasses on at all or anythying you don't want to lose.
  • Pack good sunscreen. A small tube is easier to carry than a large spray can.
  • If it's more than a few hours to travel to the park, consider getting a hotel the nght before your visit so you are not tired when you are trying to enjoy yourself.
  • Take a look at the park map before you go if you've never been there before. You'll be able to get around a lot better than trying to figure it out then.

Day of your trip

  • Eat a big, harty breakfast. Obviosuly not immediately before riding a coaster, but if you eat before you go, you won't be looking for a meal the minute you walk in and you'll save a bit because food will be quite expensive inside the park.
  • Be there early. Try to be there well before the park actually opens so you can maximize your time. Typically the earlier you arrive, the further inside the park you'll be waiting before they open the gates or remove the ropes, or remove whatever crowd control system they are using.
  • In general, try to carry as little as possible. For example, just take your car key if you drove, not the whole key ring. Take things out of your wallet you don't need for the trip in case you lose it.
  • Have a plan for which rides you want to go on first. Avoid the rides near the front of the park in the morning because the rides in the back of the park will have shorter lines for longer. For example, rides like Gatekeeper at Cedar Point are huge and exciting and you can see it from outside the park. For those reasons, the lines for that ride are almost guarenteed to be long. However you can likely hit 2 or 3 of the coasters in the back of the park by the time the park fills in with people.
  • Some rides have very strict loose article policies, and even a waist pack isn't allowed on. In these cases, there are usually lockers for that ride and they sometimes cost a few dollars to use. In some parks your locker rental can move around with your from ride to ride, but you have to remember to move your things. Some rides at some parks will let you keep items at the station, but the security from theft is not as good as lockers so use them at your own risk.
